Why Choose Braces For Your Teen?

Braces do more than just straighten teeth; they contribute significantly to overall oral health. Misaligned teeth can lead to various issues, such as difficulty biting, chewing, and even speaking. By addressing these problems, braces help improve the functionality of your teen's teeth, ensuring they can eat and speak comfortably.

Furthermore, straight teeth are easier to clean, reducing the risk of cavities and gum disease. Correcting misalignments early can prevent future dental complications and the need for more invasive treatments down the line. This proactive approach not only saves time and money but also enhances your teen's oral health in the long run.

Understanding Teens’ Orthodontic Needs

The teenage years are an ideal time for orthodontic treatment. During this period, the body is still growing, making it easier to correct dental issues and achieve lasting results. At Pitner Orthodontics, we recognize the unique advantages of this developmental stage and work to optimize treatment outcomes for each of our teen patients.

Understanding Teens’ Orthodontic Needs

As teenagers grow, their teeth and jaws are more responsive to orthodontic treatment. This responsiveness allows us to make significant adjustments more efficiently, guiding teeth into their proper positions as the mouth continues to develop.

Common Issues Addressed

During adolescence, several dental problems can arise. Orthodontic treatment can effectively address these issues, ensuring a healthy, well-aligned smile. Here are some common dental irregularities we treat:

  • Crooked Teeth: Misaligned teeth can affect both appearance and function. Braces help straighten teeth, improving overall oral health.

  • Overcrowding: When there isn’t enough space in the mouth, teeth can become crowded. Braces create the necessary space and align the teeth properly.

  • Gaps Between Teeth: Gaps can be a cosmetic concern and affect how teeth function. Orthodontic treatment closes these gaps for a more uniform smile.

  • Overbites and Underbites: Misaligned bites can cause difficulty in chewing and speaking. Braces correct these issues, promoting better bite alignment.

  • Crossbites: This occurs when some upper teeth sit inside the lower teeth instead of outside. Braces help correct crossbites, preventing uneven wear on teeth.

    • Our Braces Options For Teens

    Understanding that no two teens are alike, we provide several orthodontic solutions to ensure the best possible outcome for each individual. Here are the options we offer:

    Traditional Metal Braces

    Metal braces have long been trusted for their reliability in achieving straighter teeth. Modern advancements have made them more comfortable and less noticeable than ever. With smaller and lighter brackets, they minimize mouth irritation while effectively correcting dental alignment.

    + PROS
  • Can handle complex orthodontic issues, including severe misalignments and bite problems.
  • Made of high-quality stainless steel, they are very strong and resistant to damage.
  • Generally less expensive than other orthodontic options.
    • + CONS
  • The metal brackets and wires are visible, which might be a concern for some teens.
  • Initial irritation to the inside of the mouth and periodic tightening can cause discomfort.
  • Certain foods must be avoided to prevent damage to the braces.

    • Ceramic Braces

    Ceramic braces offer a discreet alternative to traditional metal braces, using tooth-colored or clear brackets that blend seamlessly with your teeth. They provide effective teeth straightening while being less noticeable, making them a preferred choice for teens who value aesthetics without compromising on treatment effectiveness.

    + PROS
  • The tooth-colored or clear brackets blend in with the natural color of the teeth.
  • Work similarly to metal braces, addressing a wide range of orthodontic issues.
  • More visually appealing, especially for teens concerned about their appearance.
    • + CONS
  • Generally more expensive than traditional metal braces.
  • The ceramic material can be more brittle than metal, making the brackets more prone to breaking.
  • The clear or tooth-colored brackets can stain if not properly cared for.

    • Lingual Braces

    Lingual braces are a unique option where the brackets are placed on the back of the teeth, making them virtually invisible from the front. This discreet solution is perfect for teens who might be self-conscious about wearing braces. Despite this, lingual braces require a bit more attention to oral hygiene and may take a little longer to get used to.

    + PROS
  • Placed on the back of the teeth, making them invisible from the front.
  • Can handle most orthodontic issues, similar to traditional braces.
    • + CONS
  • May cause more tongue irritation and take longer to get used to.
  • More challenging to clean, requiring meticulous oral hygiene.
  • Often more expensive and not as widely available as other options.

    How much do braces for teens cost?

    The cost of braces can vary depending on the type and complexity of the orthodontic issues. During the initial consultation, we’ll provide a personalized treatment plan with a clear breakdown of costs and discuss available payment options and insurance coverage.

    Are braces uncomfortable for teenagers?

    While some discomfort is normal, especially after adjustments, our modern braces are designed to minimize pain. We provide guidance on managing any discomfort and ensuring a smooth treatment process.

    How long does braces treatment typically take for teens?

    The duration of treatment varies based on individual needs. Most treatments last between 12 to 24 months, but this can differ depending on the specific case.

    What is the best way to care for braces?

    Proper oral hygiene is essential. We provide detailed instructions on brushing, flossing, and avoiding foods that can damage braces. We guide on how to clean and maintain the braces effectively.

    Are retainers necessary after braces treatment?

    Yes, retainers are crucial for maintaining the results achieved during treatment. They help keep the teeth in their new positions and prevent them from shifting back.
