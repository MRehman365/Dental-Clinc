When To Visit An Orthodontist

Curious about when to take your child to the orthodontist? At Pitner Orthodontics, we follow the American Association of Orthodontists' advice: a first visit by age 7. By this stage, children have a mix of baby and adult teeth, allowing us to spot and treat orthodontic issues early for healthier smiles

Early Diagnosis Benefits

+ Timely Identification of Issues
An early visit helps in spotting problems with jaw growth and emerging teeth while some baby teeth are still present. This early intervention can guide the development of the jaw and proper placement of permanent teeth.
+ Proactive Treatment Plans
If potential concerns like crowding, spacing, or misalignments are detected early, we can plan effectively. Sometimes, immediate action isn’t necessary; instead, monitoring can suffice until the optimal time for treatment.
+ Less Complicated Treatments
Early detection often results in simpler, less invasive treatments. Addressing an orthodontic issue during the developmental stages can prevent more serious problems later, reducing the need for prolonged treatment.

Our Early Orthodontic Evaluation

At Pitner Orthodontics, our early evaluations provide parents peace of mind and set children on the path to optimal oral health. During this complimentary initial visit, our team conducts a comprehensive assessment to understand your child’s unique orthodontic needs.

What To Expect During The Evaluation

+ Initial Consultation
We begin with a discussion about your child’s dental history and any concerns you might have. This conversation helps us tailor the evaluation to address specific issues and provide the most relevant advice.
+ Dental Imaging
We use state-of-the-art technology to take panoramic x-rays and digital photographs of your child’s mouth. These images are crucial as they help us see below the surface, revealing any hidden problems with tooth roots, jaw growth, and the positioning of emerging teeth.
+ Detailed Examination
Our orthodontists carefully examine your child’s teeth, jaw, and bite alignment. This comprehensive check-up helps us identify issues such as crowding, premature loss of baby teeth, or extra teeth that may affect the development of permanent teeth.s

Following The Evaluation

No Immediate Treatment Needed

If we determine that your child doesn’t require immediate treatment, we won’t recommend unnecessary procedures. Instead, we’ll suggest a monitoring plan, scheduling brief, regular check-ups to observe the development of your child’s teeth and jaws. This approach enables us to intervene at the optimal time, ensuring effective and efficient treatment when needed

Recommendations for Treatment

If early treatment is recommended, we will explain its necessity and outline the procedures involved. Our objective is to ensure that you fully comprehend all aspects of the proposed treatment, empowering you to make informed decisions regarding your child’s dental care.

Types Of Braces And Appliances For Kids

Traditional Metal Braces

Traditional metal braces are the most common type of braces for children. They consist of high-grade stainless steel brackets attached to each tooth, linked by a thin archwire that guides the teeth into place. Regular adjustments are made to this wire to continue the tooth-moving process.

Ceramic Braces

Ceramic braces function similarly to traditional metal braces but are made from ceramic materials that blend with the natural color of the teeth, making them less noticeable. These braces offer a more aesthetic alternative to metal braces while providing the same level of effectiveness. They are particularly popular among older children and teenagers who are conscious of the appearance of braces.

Lingual Braces

Lingual braces are custom-made to fit the inside surfaces of the teeth, making them invisible from the outside. They are ideal for children and teens who are concerned about the visible aspect of braces but need the functionality of traditional orthodontic treatment. Lingual braces are suitable for treating most orthodontic problems without altering a child’s appearance.

Self-Ligating Braces

Self-ligating braces use a specialized clip in place of elastics to hold the archwire. This clip helps reduce the amount of pressure exerted on the tooth and requires fewer adjustments. These braces provide shorter treatment times and require fewer visits to the orthodontist. They are easier to clean and generally more comfortable than traditional braces.

Customizable Appliances

Depending on the specific needs of your child, we might also recommend other orthodontic appliances, such as expanders, retainers, or space maintainers. These are used to address issues like narrow arches or to maintain space for permanent teeth to come in.
